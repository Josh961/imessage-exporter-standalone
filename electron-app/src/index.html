About iMessage Exporter
This software is open source and based on the code from ReagentX/imessage-exporter. This version is packaged as a standalone alternative, also open source, and can be found at Josh961/imessage-exporter-standalone. Both projects are licensed under the GPL-3.0 License. The installed executable is bundled with ImageMagick. ImageMagick is licensed under the ImageMagick License, which can be found at https://imagemagick.org/script/license.php.

Export settings
Slow export (more thorough)

Uses an alternative method that's more thorough when standard contact filtering encounters issues. Takes longer but ensures your selected contacts are exported.

Full export (no contact filtering)

Export all messages without selecting contacts. This will export your entire message history within the specified date range.

Select iPhone Backup

No iPhone backups found

To create a backup:

  1. Connect your iPhone to your Mac with a cable
  2. In the Finder sidebar, select your iPhone
  3. At the top of the Finder window, click General
  4. Select "Back up all of the data on your iPhone to this Mac"
    • Ensure the "Encrypt local backup" option is unchecked
  5. Click Back Up Now
  1. Connect your iPhone to your computer using a USB cable
  2. In the iTunes app, click the Device button near the top left of the iTunes window
  3. Click Summary
  4. Click Back Up Now (below Backups)
    • Do not save the backup to iCloud. Save locally to your computer
    • Ensure the "Encrypt local backup" option is unchecked
  5. Wait for the backup to complete

After the backup completes, close and reopen this dialog to see your backup.

Note: If you do not have iTunes installed, you can download it from Apple's website

What you can do:

  • Check the date range shown in the "Select contacts" popup for your selected contact - the date range you selected may be outside the range of messages we found
  • If the date range lines up with what is shown in the "Select contacts" popup and the export finishes instantly, try exporting with the "slow export method" by closing this dialog, clicking the cog wheel in the top right, and clicking "Export with slow method". This will take longer but will ensure your selected contact is exported.
  • If the date range shown in the "Select contacts" popup doesn't go back far enough to the messages you expect, but you can see them in your iPhone, create a manual backup to reliably export them (see below).
  1. Connect your iPhone to your Mac with a cable
  2. In the Finder sidebar, select your iPhone
  3. At the top of the Finder window, click General
  4. Select "Back up all of the data on your iPhone to this Mac"
    • Ensure the "Encrypt local backup" option is unchecked
  5. Click Back Up Now
  6. After backup completes, close this dialog and change the iMessage Backup Folder to use your new backup
  1. Connect your iPhone to your computer using a USB cable
  2. In the iTunes app, click the Device button near the top left of the iTunes window
  3. Click Summary
  4. Click Back Up Now (below Backups)
    • Do not save the backup to iCloud. Save locally to your computer
    • Ensure the "Encrypt local backup" option is unchecked
  5. Wait for the backup to complete
  6. After backup completes, close this dialog and change the iMessage Backup Folder to use your new backup

If you can see your messages on your iPhone, then creating a backup will reliably export them. The Messages app on your Mac may not always be fully synced with your iPhone.
